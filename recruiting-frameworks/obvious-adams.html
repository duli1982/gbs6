Obvious Adams

Your BS Filter for Recruiting

Say the obvious truth. Cut the corporate speak. Focus on what candidates and hiring managers actually care about.

What Is Obvious Adams?

Obvious Adams is your "BS filter" on top of all the other frameworks.

AIDA, PAS, etc. tell you how to structure a message. Obvious Adams tells you:

"Are we actually saying the obvious, true, useful thing the candidate/HM cares about or are we being clever and corporate again?"

How Obvious Adams Fits Into 20-30-50

In the 203050 model, Obvious Adams mainly lives in the 50% Human & Brand part, but it also touches 20 and 30:

20% Search Craft

Obvious roles, obvious talent pools first. Don't over-engineer "niche" until the basics are covered.

30% AI Assist

Use AI to generate ideas, then apply Obvious Adams: "What is the simplest, most honest version of this?"

50% Human & Brand

Every outreach, JD, LinkedIn post and HM email should pass the Obvious Adams test:

  • Are we naming the obvious problem?
  • Are we saying the obvious truth everyone feels but nobody writes?
  • Are we offering the obvious next step?

So it's less a "copy structure" and more a decision + clarity framework.

The 5 Obvious Questions

Ask yourself these before sending anything

1

Obvious Problem

What is the most obvious pain this candidate/HM has right now?

Example: "You're probably drowning in routine lab work with no time for your own research."

2

Obvious Truth

What is the blunt, honest thing everyone knows but we rarely say?

Example: "Let's be honest: this isn't a 9 to 5. But you get real ownership and a manager who actually backs you."

3

Obvious Fix

What is the simple way this role/team actually improves that?

Example: "This team gives you protected time for your own experiments, not just service work."

4

Obvious Proof

What is one concrete fact / example that shows it's real, not marketing?

Example: "The last scientist we hired published 2 papers in their first year from their own ideas, not assigned projects."

5

Obvious Next Step

What's the smallest, clearest next step?

Example: "Reply 'yes' or 'no', or I can send you 3 bullet points so you can decide in 30 seconds."

Rule of Thumb:

If your message doesn't hit at least 3 of these 5, it's probably fluff.

Concrete Before/After with Obvious Adams

Scenario: Outreach to a senior scientist

BEFORE (typical recruiter fluff):

"I'd love to present you with an exciting opportunity at a leading global organization. We offer a competitive package and a dynamic environment."

Run Obvious Adams:

  • Obvious Problem? candidate is tired of vague promises, no clarity
  • Obvious Truth? job will be demanding, but growth + ownership are real
  • Obvious Fix? clearer scope, better manager, less chaos
  • Obvious Proof? small team, real examples, not just buzzwords
  • Obvious Next Step? small, easy: "can send 3 bullets / say no"

AFTER (Obvious Adams version):

"Let me be straight: this isn't a chill 95. You'd own a tough portfolio but you'd also get a manager who shields you from nonsense and actually backs your decisions.

If you want, I can send 3 bullet points so you can decide in 30 seconds if it's worth a chat."

Result:

Same role, totally different trust signal.

How to Use Obvious Adams

1

Write First

Use any framework (AIDA, PAS, etc.) to write your message

2

Apply the Filter

Run through the 5 Obvious Questions hit at least 3

3

Simplify & Send

Cut corporate speak, say the obvious truth, make it human

Key Principle:

If you wouldn't say it to someone over coffee, don't write it in an email.
