8 Proven Structures for Modern Recruiting

Master these frameworks for outreach, job ads, LinkedIn posts, and HM communications that actually get responses.

Why These Frameworks Matter

These aren't just copywriting tricks they're tested structures that help you write messages people actually read and respond to.

Start with the basics first (Bar Test, 3-Line Outreach, Jargon Swap), then level up with these.

1

AIDA Attention Interest Desire Action

For: Job ads, LinkedIn posts, outreach where you need to "pull" someone in step by step

Idea: Don't start with "We have an exciting opportunity." Start with something that hooks attention, then slowly earn the right to ask for action.

Attention

Hook, pattern break

Interest

Relevant to them

Desire

What life looks like

Action

Clear next step

Outreach Example:

  • A: "Most scientists I speak with are drowning in lab work and still stuck in old equipment."
  • I: "Your experience in biologics + setting up new methods really stood out you're basically already doing half of what this team needs."
  • D: "This role gives you modern kit, budget, and a lead scientist who actually listens when you push for change."
  • A: "If you're open, I can send 3 bullet points. A 'no' is also completely fine."
2

PAS Problem Agitate Solution

For: Cold outreach and job ads that speak to pain points (burnout, stuck roles, zero growth, chaos)

Idea: People move to get away from pain just as much as to chase something better.

Problem

Name what's hurting

Agitate

Dig deeper

Solution

Show the way out

Outreach Example:

  • P: "A lot of senior sourcers tell me they're treated like CV machines."
  • A: "No say in role design, no real partnership with hiring managers just 'send more profiles'."
  • S: "I'm working with a team where sourcers push back, influence the process, and get measured on quality, not volume. Want a 10-min chat to see if it's your kind of environment?"
3

BAB Before After Bridge

For: LinkedIn posts, storytelling in outreach, "why this role" parts of job ads

Idea: Show the transformation how life looks now vs. how it could look.

Before

Current reality

After

Improved reality

Bridge

The path

Job Ad Intro Example:

  • Before: "Right now you're probably juggling experiments, firefighting, and reporting to three different people."
  • After: "Imagine one clear scope, a PI who shields you from noise, and time to actually think about your data."
  • Bridge: "That's the setup in this Senior Scientist role in Darmstadt."
4

FAB Features Advantages Benefits

For: Turning boring job bullet points into candidate-relevant benefits

Idea: Nobody cares that you have "modern lab infrastructure." They care what that does for them.

Feature

The thing

Advantage

Why it's better

Benefit

What it changes

Example:

  • F: "On-site, state-of-the-art bioconjugation lab."
  • A: "You don't wait weeks for access or queue for basic equipment."
  • B: "You get to run more of your ideas, not just chase backlog."

"Access to modern facilities"

"You'll work in a modern bioconjugation lab, which means less waiting, more time running your own experiments, and faster turnaround on results."

5

WIIFM "What's In It For Me?" Filter

For: EVERYTHING - Outreach, job ads, InMails, LinkedIn posts, HM emails

Idea: Every line should answer one of their questions, not yours.

For candidates, WIIFM =

  • Will my work matter?
  • Will this help my career?
  • Will my life be better (money, time, stress, learning)?
  • Will I be treated decently?

How to use it:

  1. 1. Write your message
  2. 2. Ask: "If I were the candidate, what do I get from this?"
  3. 3. If the answer is "company branding / blah-blah," rewrite.

"We are a leading global organization offering a competitive package."

"You'd join a small team inside a big company lots of stability, but you still know everyone you work with by name."

6

4U's Useful, Urgent, Unique, Ultra-specific

For: Subject lines, first lines of InMails, job ad headings, LinkedIn hooks

Idea: You don't need all 4 every time but aim for 23.

Useful

"What do I get?"

Urgent

"Why now?"

Unique

"Why different?"

Ultra-specific

Numbers, detail

Subject Line Examples:

  • "Bioconjugation role where you design the strategy, not just follow it" (Useful + Unique)
  • "2-liner about a role that fixes the 'no time for real science' problem" (Useful + Ultra-specific)
  • "Quick question about your move after your PhD" (Useful + Urgent + Specific)
7

HSO Hook Story Offer

For: LinkedIn posts, outreach that feels like a micro-story instead of a pitch

Hook

Strong opening

Story

Short anecdote

Offer

Invite, not sell

LinkedIn Post Example:

Hook: "I don't care how smart your Boolean is if your candidate experience is trash."

Story: "Last month I spoke to a candidate who had 3 recruiters ghost her after final interview. She remembers their names. She will never take their calls again. Our tools are getting smarter. Our behavior? Not always."

Offer: "If you lead a team, your edge in 2025 is not 'more reach' it's 'better follow-through'. Happy to share how we're trying to fix this in our team."

8

PASTOR Problem Amplify Story Transformation Offer Response

For: Senior/hard-to-fill roles, landing pages, bigger LinkedIn posts

Idea: Longer-form but powerful for important roles and campaigns

Very Short Version in Outreach:

  • P: "A lot of Heads of TA feel squeezed between business demands and hiring reality."
  • A: "You get the blame, but you don't get the tools or headcount."
  • S/T: "The TA Director here joined 2 years ago in that exact setup. Today, they've doubled internal mobility and cut agency spend by 30%."
  • O/R: "If you're curious how that change happened, I can walk you through it in 15 minutes no obligation, just a comparison point."

How to teach this to your team:

"We'll mostly use three: AIDA for structure, PAS for pain, and WIIFM as a filter. The others are bonus tools."

How to Use These Frameworks

1

Pick Your Framework

Choose based on your goal: outreach, job ad, LinkedIn post, or subject line

2

Follow the Structure

Fill in each component don't skip steps or rush the flow

3

Test & Refine

Read it out loud, apply the Bar Test, get feedback from your team

Pro Tip:

Start with AIDA, PAS, and WIIFM. Master these three before exploring the rest.
