3-Minute Search Checklists

Start Right, Fix Fast

Two quick checklists: one before you search, one when you're stuck. Use them every time.

🧭 3-Minute Pre-Search Checklist

Before you open LinkedIn / CVDB / AI

Use this to start every search right

1

Role DNA Sanity Check

6090 seconds

Ask yourself:

1

Do I have 34 clear must-haves written down?

  • Skills / technologies / domain (e.g. ADC, GMP, B2B, etc.)
2

Did I write 12 lines of context?

  • Industry, team type, lab vs office, level (junior/medior/senior)
3

Do I know the flex zones?

  • What can we bend on? years of experience, industry, exact title?

⚠ If it's all still in your head → write a 57 bullet search brief first.

2

Titles & Synonyms

60 seconds

4

Have I listed at least:

  • 23 obvious titles?
  • 23 alternative/hidden titles? (e.g. Scientist / Research Associate / Engineer)
5

Did I think about company types where this person may sit?

  • Big pharma / CRO / biotech / vendor / consultancy?

💡 Optional AI Assist:

"Here's my role brief. Suggest 5 alternative job titles and 10 keywords I should include in my search."
3

Search Shape

60 seconds

6

What will be my first search angle?

  • Title + key skill?
  • Company-based?
  • Skill-based?
7

Is my location logic realistic?

  • Commutable area? Hybrid? Remote? Any relocation?
8

Have I picked one starter template from our team skeletons?

  • Lab / commercial / tech starter then adapt
4

Ready to Search?

30 seconds

Quick self-check:

9

Can I explain this role in one sentence to a candidate?

10

If I had to brief AI in 23 lines, would it be clear?

If YES → you're ready. 🚀

If NO → your problem is not Boolean, it's the brief. Fix that first.

🧭 3-Minute Stuck-Search Checklist

When you're 1015 minutes in and thinking "there are no candidates"

Use this whenever:

  • › You get 020 results in a normal market, or
  • › You get tons of junk, or
  • › All profiles feel "almost but not quite right"
1

Check the Brief, Not the String

60 seconds

1

Did I over-translate HM fantasy into must-haves?

  • Can one person realistically have all the things we listed?
2

Is something that should be a flex zone sitting as a hard filter?

  • Exact industry? Exact tech stack? Exact company type?

If yes → relax 12 things and see what changes.

2

Widen Titles & Keywords

60 seconds

3

Do I only have 12 titles in my string?

  • Add 23 more realistic variations (Senior / Lead / Associate etc.)
4

Am I using too specific jargon that only exists at one company?

  • Replace with more generic skills / methods / tools
5

Ask AI quickly:

"Here is my current search and role brief. Suggest 5 more job titles and 10 related keywords I might be missing."

Update and rerun.

3

Check Geography & Filters

60 seconds

6

Is my location filter killing the search?

  • Try: city + region + "within X km" / neighboring cities
7

Am I stacking too many filters at once?

  • Seniority + industry + years + education + language = death
  • Temporarily remove 12 filters and see if real people appear
4

Flip the Angle

4560 seconds

8

Have I tried another search angle?

From skill-based to company-based:

  • "Show me people at 510 target companies doing something close"

Or from title-only to skill + environment:

  • "GMP + bioconjugation + 'lab'" instead of just job titles
9

Did I look at who's already in process for similar roles?

  • Reuse patterns: where did those people come from? which titles?
5

Escalate or Reset

30 seconds

10

If after trying the above it's still bad:

› Drop 23 screenshots of the best you can find into Teams/Slack

› Message:

"This is what the market is giving us with current must-haves. To open it up, we might need to flex on X or Y. Can we discuss?"

💡 Remember:

Sometimes it's not a search problem, it's a requirements problem and that's part of the job too.

Use These Every Time

Pre-search keeps you focused. Stuck-search gets you unstuck. Both save hours of wasted effort.
