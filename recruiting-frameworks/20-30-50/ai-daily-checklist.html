Daily AI Checklist

ðŸ§©How to Use AI in Your Day

The Minimum Expected from Everyone

A simple, actionable checklist for sourcers & recruiters. Drop this into your workflow and make AI work for you.

This is not optional. This is the baseline. If you're not doing at least half of these daily, you're working too hard.

1

Before You Start Search

Set Up the Role

Turn JD + notes into a clear brief

Paste JD + your notes into AI. Ask:

"Turn this into a 1-page search brief: context, must-haves, nice-to-haves, target companies, titles to target, profiles to avoid."

Ask for title & keyword ideas

"Give me 10 job titles and 10 keywords I should search for this role in [country/region]."

Rule: If you're starting from a blank page you've already skipped a step.

2

While You Search

Let AI Build the Skeleton

Get AI to write your first Boolean / X-ray

"Create 3 LinkedIn Boolean strings for this role. Keep each on one line. Focus on [seniority] in [location]."

Ask for adjacent ideas

"What adjacent roles or industries might have people who fit this role but don't have the exact title?"

Sanity-check your search

"Here is my current search logic. What might be missing or too narrow for this role?"

Remember: You still own the final version, but AI makes version 1 and 2 for you.

3

Before You Write Outreach

Stop Starting from Zero

Use AI for 1st draft of outreach

Paste role summary + candidate profile.

"Write 2 short outreach messages (max 5 sentences) to this candidate about this role. Tone: human, direct, no corporate fluff. End with a simple question."

Use AI for follow-up

"Write 1 follow-up message if the candidate hasn't replied in 7 days. Keep it light, respectful, and under 4 sentences."

Your rule: AI drafts you shorten, add 1 real detail from the profile, and make it sound like you.

4

After Calls

Don't Manually Fight with Notes

Summarize candidate calls

"Summarize this candidate call in bullet points: motivation, key skills, risks/concerns, salary expectations, location/relocation, notice period. Max 12 bullets."

Turn this into HM-ready feedback

"Turn this into a short email update for the hiring manager: 35 bullets, clear recommendation (move forward / reject / hold) and 23 questions for HM if needed."

Build quick scorecards

"Based on this role brief, create a simple 5-criteria scorecard (15 scale) I can use to rate candidates after screening."
5

After Intake / HM Calls

Make Structure, Not Chaos

Clean up intake

Paste messy HM notes.

"Turn this into a structured intake summary: context, must-haves, nice-to-haves, target companies, profiles to avoid, 5 screening questions, 3 risk areas to watch."

Ask AI what's unclear

"From this summary, list what is still unclear or risky and should be confirmed with the HM."

Result: Next time you talk to HM, you have specific questions, not "do you have any updates?"

6

When You Have Profiles

Let AI Help You Prioritize

Use AI for shortlisting

Paste 815 profile snippets.

"From these profiles, rank the top 10 to contact first for this role. For each, give 12 lines: why they're a fit and what to double-check on the call."

Remember: You still decide, but you save brain energy on first pass.

7

When You're Stuck

Get AI to Challenge Your Thinking

Use AI as a thinking partner

Ask questions like:

  • "What might I be missing in my search strategy for this role?"
  • "What are 5 different angles I could use in outreach to this type of candidate?"
  • "How could I reframe this role to make it more attractive without lying?"
8

Guardrails

How NOT to Use AI

Never copy-paste without reading

If you can't stand behind the message or summary don't send it.

No inventing facts

Always tell AI:

"If information is missing, don't make it up. Just say it's missing."

Protect candidate trust

  • Don't paste sensitive info with names if not needed.
  • Remove anything you wouldn't want in writing somewhere else.

This Is The Baseline

If you're doing at least 4 of these 8 things daily, you're ahead of 80% of recruiters.
