## OBJECTION HANDLING

### **"Can't they just use ChatGPT for free?"**
*"Yes, and they can also Google everything instead of going to university. But they won't become experts. We provide structured learning, expert prompts tested on real recruitment scenarios, and personalized roadmaps. ChatGPT is a tool. We teach mastery."*

### **"How is this different from LinkedIn Learning?"**
*"LinkedIn Learning teaches theory. We teach application. Our users save 15 hours per week in their first month. LinkedIn courses take months to complete and rarely get applied. We're outcome-driven, not course-completion-driven."*

### **"This feels like a side project, not a business unit."**
*"We have 1,000+ active users, documented $152M in annual value, multiple revenue streams ready to activate, and a clear path to $50M ARR. Side projects don't generate $152M in value. This is a business."*

### **"Why not just license this to a training company?"**
*"Because we'd be leaving $100M+ on the table. We own the IP, the distribution, the customer relationships, and the content. Licensing gets us 5-10% royalties. Owning it gets us 100% of the upside."*

### **"How do you compete with Google offering Gemini training for free?"**
*"Google teaches Gemini. We teach recruiters how to use Gemini to save 15 hours per week. Big difference. Google wants adoption. We drive ROI. They'll partner with us or acquire us, not compete with us."*

### **"What if AI makes recruiters obsolete?"**
*"AI won't replace recruiters. AI-powered recruiters will replace recruiters who don't use AI. We're on the right side of that equation. We're making our people indispensable."*
