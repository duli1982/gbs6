üèóÔ∏è Enterprise AI Architecture Patterns

Understanding these architectural patterns is crucial for successful enterprise AI integration:

üåê Hub-and-Spoke Model

Central AI hub serves multiple business units with standardized interfaces.

Best For: Large organizations with diverse departments
Benefits: Centralized governance, consistent quality, cost efficiency
Challenges: Potential bottlenecks, customization limitations

üîó Federated Integration

Distributed AI capabilities with shared standards and governance.

Best For: Organizations with autonomous business units
Benefits: Flexibility, local optimization, faster deployment
Challenges: Governance complexity, potential inconsistencies

üöÄ Microservices Architecture

AI capabilities deployed as independent, scalable microservices.

Best For: Cloud-native organizations with DevOps maturity
Benefits: Scalability, resilience, independent deployment
Challenges: Complexity, monitoring overhead, service coordination

üîÑ Event-Driven Integration

AI processes triggered by business events and data changes.

Best For: Real-time processing and reactive systems
Benefits: Real-time response, loose coupling, scalability
Challenges: Event management, debugging complexity
