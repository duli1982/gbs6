Fundamentals (1-10)

Core principles for effective prompt construction

1

Be Direct and Clear

Avoid polite language like "please" or "if you don't mind." Get straight to the task with clear, direct instructions.

‚úÖ Good Example:

"Explain the causes of climate change in simple terms."

‚ùå Avoid:

"Could you please explain climate change if you don't mind?"

2

Specify the Audience and Role

Assign Gemini a specific role to focus the response on your target audience.

‚úÖ Example:

"You are a financial advisor for small business owners. Explain a savings strategy for someone starting a business."

3

Break Down Complex Tasks

Use sequential, interactive prompts for clarity when dealing with complex topics.

‚úÖ Example:

"Explain machine learning basics. Follow up with examples of its real-world applications."

4

Use Affirmative Directives

Phrase instructions positively using "Do" rather than "Don't."

‚úÖ Good:

"Provide an engaging introduction to AI ethics."

‚ùå Avoid:

"Don't make it overly technical."

5

Utilize Instruction Tokens for Structure

Organize prompts with clear indicators for clarity and advanced outputs.

‚úÖ Example:

Role: You are a historian explaining ancient Greek philosophy.
Task: Summarize the key ideas of Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle in under 500 words.

6

Include Few-Shot Examples

Provide examples of the desired format or tone to guide Gemini's response.

‚úÖ Example:

"Write an email proposal. Example: 'Dear [Name], I am reaching out regarding...' Now, write a similar email for a marketing proposal."

7

Control Length Explicitly

Specify the desired response length to get appropriately sized outputs.

‚úÖ Examples:

‚Ä¢ "Summarize this article in 3 bullet points."
‚Ä¢ "Write a 300-word description of Gemini's capabilities."

8

Use Instructional Keywords

Include phrases like 'Your task is' or 'You MUST' to emphasize requirements.

‚úÖ Example:

"Your task is to analyze this data and explain its key insights."

9

Encourage Iterative Clarification

Allow Gemini to ask clarifying questions if the task is ambiguous.

‚úÖ Example:

"From now on, ask questions to gather all details before generating outputs."

10

Leverage Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Ask Gemini to think step by step for logical, well-reasoned outputs.

‚úÖ Example:

"Explain how photosynthesis works, step by step."

Structure & Format (11-20)

Organizing prompts for maximum clarity and effectiveness

11

Use Indicators to Focus Context

Separate instructions, examples, and input with clear indicators.

‚úÖ Example:

Task: Analyze this text for its historical accuracy.
Text: The Declaration of Independence was signed in 1776.

12

Combine Techniques

Pair Chain-of-Thought with Few-Shot examples for complex tasks.

‚úÖ Example:

"Think step by step to solve this logic puzzle. Here's an example of how to approach it..."

13

Ask for Unbiased Responses

Ensure outputs are free of stereotypes or bias by explicitly requesting neutrality.

‚úÖ Example:

"Ensure your explanation is unbiased and factual."

14

Optimize Token Usage

Keep prompts concise while providing enough context for accurate responses.

‚úÖ Example:

"Summarize this 1,000-word document in 5 sentences."

15

Add Output Primers

End prompts with the start of the desired output to guide the response format.

‚úÖ Example:

"A successful marketing strategy includes..."

16

Pretend Like You're Tipping Your AI

Add motivational language to encourage better performance (experimental technique).

‚úÖ Example:

"I'm going to tip $20 for a better solution!"

17

Specify Style and Format

Define tone, structure, or style requirements explicitly.

‚úÖ Examples:

‚Ä¢ "Write a humorous blog post about technology."
‚Ä¢ "Format your response as a Q&A dialogue."

18

Combine Chain-of-Thought with Few-Shot Examples

Guide reasoning with a logical step-by-step approach, using examples for context.

‚úÖ Example:

"Think step by step: Explain the causes of inflation. Example: 'Rising demand can outpace supply...' Now explain other causes."

19

Use Follow-Up Questions for Refinement

Refine initial outputs with targeted follow-up questions.

‚úÖ Example:

"Great summary. Can you simplify this explanation for a high school audience?"

20

Leverage Multi-Modal Inputs

Add images, documents, or other media to improve your prompt when necessary.

‚úÖ Example:

"Write an imaginary background story for this cat shown in photo. Come up with a reason why it might be hiding there and why its expression is that way."
