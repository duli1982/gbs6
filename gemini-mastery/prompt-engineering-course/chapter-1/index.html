üìù 1.3 Beginner Level Prompts Illustrating Each Principle

Now that we've covered the core principles, let's see them in action. With Gemini, simply communicate naturally‚Äîno XML tagging needed.

Clarity

Straightforward and unambiguous

Describe that process of Protein Synthesis in simple terms.

Easy to understand with no vagueness.

Specificity

Includes precise details and criteria

You are a sports analyst with expertise in Major League Baseball. List five of the most iconic U.S. baseball players from the 1990s, based on their performance, popularity, and cultural impact during that decade.

Defines role, timeframe, criteria, and format clearly.

Context Setting

Establishes role and context

You are a social media influencer. In the context of social media marketing, explain the importance of authenticity of content creation.

Both role and context ensure relevant, consistent output.

Instructiveness

Clear directions with constraints

Rewrite the following paragraph to make it more concise and professional, keeping the original meaning intact and limiting it to 3 sentences: "Our team was really excited about the new product launch, and we worked super hard to get everything ready in time. There were some last-minute issues, but we managed to fix them before the deadline. Everyone put in a lot of effort and showed real dedication."

Specifies action, approach, and constraints.

Balanced Prompting

Simple vs. complex based on need

Simple:

Explain what supply and demand means in basic terms.

Advanced:

Explain the concept of supply and demand in simple terms for someone new to economics. Then, give two real-life examples: one showing what happens when demand is high but supply is low, and another showing the opposite. Keep your explanation clear and relatable.

Match complexity to your goal.

Iterative Refinement

Build on previous responses

Step 1:

Summarize the main idea of the article below in one sentence. [insert article]

Step 2:

Now expand that summary into a short paragraph that includes the article's main argument, one supporting point, and the author's conclusion. Keep it under 100 words and maintain a neutral tone.

Chain prompts for deeper, controlled results.

üí° 1.4 Tips and Tricks to Remember These Principles

Practical advice to help you internalize effective prompt design, focusing on role assignment and iterative refinement.

1

Create a Checklist

Ensure your prompts align with core principles and avoid common pitfalls.

Before finalizing, ask:

  • ‚Ä¢ Is the task clearly defined?
  • ‚Ä¢ Are instructions specific?
  • ‚Ä¢ Have I provided context or role?
2

Use Templates

Save time with consistent structures for recurring tasks.

Role: You are a private investigator with a keen eye for detail.

Task: Analyze the following document to spot any inconsistency in the suspect's alibi.

[upload file]

3

Practice Regularly

Start with simple tasks, gradually move to complex ones. Track what works and why.

4

Leverage Role Assignment

Experiment with related roles to explore different perspectives.

[Choose one]

You are a data analyst.

You are an economist.

You are a business mogul.

Explain the trend in the following dataset: [data]

5

Iterate and Refine

Start simple, analyze responses, and improve over time.

Initial:

Explain climate change.

Better:

You are a climatologist. Explain climate change in two paragraphs, focusing on causes and effects.

Best:

You are a climatologist. Write a structured essay on climate change: what it is, how it developed, current measures, individual actions, and future projections if no action is taken.

6

Practice Critical Thinking When Chaining

Verify claims and spot inconsistencies before building on responses.

Step 1: Summarize this article: [text]

Step 2: You are a fact-checker. Verify the summary's accuracy and explain potential causes of any inaccuracies.

7

Ask for Feedback

Share prompts with peers or run outputs through quality reviews to spot improvements.
